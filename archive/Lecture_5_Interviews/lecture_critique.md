# Lecture 5 (Old) — Interview Data Collection

**Status:** From an earlier version of the course. Not in the current syllabus.
**Recommended action:** Move to `archive/`.

---

## Content Summary

This lecture covers interview data as a method: what interview data is, why collect it, do's and don'ts, interview guide design, sampling, recruitment, and modes (face-to-face, phone, Zoom, email, chat, diary).

## Why It Was Removed (Likely)

The current syllabus compresses the data collection block into a single lecture (L4: Data Design and Data Collection) focused on large-scale digital trace data. Interview methods were dropped, possibly due to time constraints.

## Recommendation

The content here is high-quality and fills a genuine gap. Even a 20-minute segment on "when and why to do interviews to ground your digital analysis" would be valuable in the current course — especially given that Grigoropoulou & Small (2022) in L2 explicitly argues for offline qualitative methods to complement digital data. Consider re-integrating a condensed version as a standalone segment within Lecture 3 (datasites) or as an optional supervision resource.

The modes map (`modes_map.png`) and the discussion of low-cost and traceable interview modes are particularly valuable for students designing mixed digital-offline studies.
