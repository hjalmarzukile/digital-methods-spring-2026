## Content Summary

This lecture provides foundational background for text analysis: text as qualitative data, large-scale text data characteristics, what people do with text (framing, evaluating, self-presentation, narrating), deductive vs. inductive approaches, grounded theory and its problems, computational grounded theory (Nelson's three-stage model: discovery, refinement, validation), and LDA/topic model problems. It ends with a midway course evaluation.

## Why It Was Removed (Likely)

This conceptual background was presumably absorbed into the current Lecture 5 (computer-assisted discovery and open coding). However, comparing the old Lecture 8 slides with the current Lecture 9 (now Lecture 5) slides, it is clear that most of this background material was *not* carried forward. The current Lecture 5 jumps straight into discovery methods without the conceptual foundation.

## Recommendation

This is the most important archive lecture for re-integration. The following sections should be incorporated as an opening section in the current `Lecture_05` slides:

1. **What people do with text** (framing, evaluating, self-presentation, narrating) — orients students to *what* they are looking for before teaching them *how*
2. **Deductive vs. inductive approaches** — the conceptual fork that motivates the computer-assisted approach
3. **Computational grounded theory overview** (discovery → refinement → validation) — the framework that Carlsen & Ralund (2022) builds on
4. **Measurement problems in topic models** — why direct validation is necessary
