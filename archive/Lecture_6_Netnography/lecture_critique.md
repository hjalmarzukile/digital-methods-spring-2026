# Lecture 6 (Old) — Netnography

**Status:** From an earlier version of the course. Not in the current syllabus as a standalone lecture.
**Recommended action:** Move to `archive/`.

---

## Content Summary

This lecture covers: what ethnography is, what ethnographers record (social interaction, setting, actors, researcher reflections), what netnography is, how it differs from ethnography (text-mediated interaction, interfaces, profiles, navigating archives), and the immersive operations framework (reconnoitering, recording, researching, reflecting), including guidance on the immersion journal.

## Why It Was Removed (Likely)

Netnography was integrated into the readings of Lecture 3 (Kozinets Ch. 8 and 10) rather than receiving a dedicated lecture, presumably due to time compression.

## Recommendation

This is a strong lecture and netnography is one of the course's three core methods (alongside content analysis and network analysis). Its removal as a dedicated lecture is a significant loss. Consider one of:

1. Re-integrating as a second session within the Lecture 3 block (3a: finding datasites; 3b: netnography)
2. Absorbing the core netnography concepts (immersive operations, recording in the journal) into the Lecture 3 slides as a condensed section
3. Making the immersion journal section more prominent in supervision sessions, drawing on this material

The "changes in what is observed" and "changes in how we observe" sections are particularly valuable for orienting students to the differences between ethnography and netnography.
