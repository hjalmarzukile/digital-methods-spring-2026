## What Works

- The five-step investigation process (simplification → search → scouting → evaluation → selection) is clear and actionable.
- The prepping/survivalism example gives a concrete walkthrough of the search-and-scout process that students can replicate.
- The ethics section using the Kozinets flowchart is appropriate and well-placed.
- Venturini et al.'s reality checklist is one of the best tools in the course and well chosen.
- In-class group exercises are well distributed throughout the lecture.

---

## Critiques

1. **Heaviest reading load in the course (~99 pages).** Kozinets Ch. 8 (31 pp.) + Ch. 10 (29 pp.) + Ch. 6 ethics (25 pp.) + Venturini et al. (14 pp.) = 99 pages for a single lecture. This is excessive and likely results in surface-level engagement with all texts.

2. **Kozinets Ch. 10 (immersion journal) is misaligned.** This chapter is assigned here but barely used in the slides — its content maps to the netnography material (old Lecture 6) that has been removed from the current syllabus. Move it to Lecture 5 or 6 where it is actually applied.

3. **Immersion journal section is compressed.** Students are expected to start an immersion journal this week, but the section covers it in 2 slides. This needs more scaffolding: what does a good journal entry look like vs. a weak one? Show examples.

4. **Venturini's checklist is presented too abstractly.** The in-class exercise ("choose and answer 1-2 questions") would benefit from a worked example first, before groups attempt it independently.

5. **Ethics of representation is absent.** The ethics section focuses on data collection ethics (consent, sensitive topics, vulnerable populations) but does not touch on the ethics of representing online subjects in publications. This gap is most visible in the outro lecture — it could be seeded here.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Given the reading load, consider splitting:
- Lecture 3a: Finding and scouting datasites (steps 1–3 of the investigation process, with the prepping example as the walkthrough)
- Lecture 3b (or supervision): Evaluation, ethics, and starting the immersion journal

Alternatively, keep the single lecture but redistribute readings: assign only Kozinets Ch. 8 + Venturini et al. here, and move Kozinets Ch. 10 to Lecture 5 and Ch. 6 to a supervision ethics session.
