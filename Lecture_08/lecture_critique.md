## What Works

- The confirmatory vs. complementary operations framework (when used here as an analytical design question, not just a data collection question) is well-applied.
- The two sequential design diagrams (simple and hierarchical/nested) are useful visual tools for students finalising their analysis plans.
- The "what role does each analysis play?" questions are well-structured and directly applicable to students' own projects.

---

## Critiques

1. **Substantial repetition of earlier content.** The confirmatory/complementary framework and sequential design diagrams have appeared in Lectures 1, 4, and now 8. If kept here, this repetition needs to do new work — the slides should not just re-present but apply the framework to students' specific analytical choices.

2. **"Insert different types of coding" placeholder is unresolved.** The slide with the text "Insert different types of coding" is a leftover placeholder. Either fill it with actual content or remove it.

3. **No readings assigned.** Syllabus Lecture 8 says "TBA" for readings. This is a gap — this lecture needs supporting readings, especially for the analytical design and quality dimensions.

4. **Lecture is short relative to its importance.** Students at this point in the course are finalising their analytical designs for the exam. A more interactive session — where groups present their current design and get class feedback — would be more valuable than a brief conceptual recap.

5. **Forward pointer to Lecture 8b missing.** The note "For Tuesday: talk about quality of inference..." at the end assumes students know what is coming. A clear statement of what Lecture 8b covers would help students prepare.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Reframe this session as a design clinic:
- Brief recap of the confirmatory/complementary framework (10 min)
- Structured group exercise: each group maps their own analysis onto the sequential design diagram and identifies the role and justification for each analysis component
- Small-group presentations and peer feedback (remaining time)
- Close with a preview of the quality criteria to be covered in Lecture 8b
