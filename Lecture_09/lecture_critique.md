## What Works

- Small's six criteria for qualitative inference (exposure, cognitive empathy, heterogeneity, palpability, follow-up, self-awareness) are outstanding and underused in methods training. This is one of the strongest conceptual contributions in the course.
- The worked example of a qualitative analysis section build-up (main claim → index case → analysis → variation → least-likely case) is excellent and directly applicable.
- The full exam prototype walkthrough (sections 1–8 with content guidance) is clear and useful.
- The data quality framework connecting datasite choice → data collection → qualitative analysis → quantitative transformation is a strong synthesis of the whole course.

---

## Critiques

1. **Six criteria appear late.** Small's criteria (especially cognitive empathy, heterogeneity, palpability) are most useful when students are still in the coding and writing phase — Lecture 5 or 6 would be a better home. Appearing here, they are more evaluative than generative. Consider moving the core criteria earlier and using this lecture for application/review.

2. **Research design with data quality (`research_design_data_quality.png`) needs a text version.** This diagram is one of the most integrative visuals in the course and should be accompanied by a bulleted explanation so it works without the graphic.

3. **The "qualitative literacy" section is underdeveloped.** The observation that quantitative literacy has outpaced qualitative literacy is important but gets only two slides. This argument deserves a short reading.

4. **Exam prep content is repeated across Lectures 12 and 13 (Outro).** The exam prototype and "must haves" appear in both lectures nearly identically. Consolidate: keep the full prototype here and keep only the oral exam guidance in the Outro.

5. **The providing-good-qualitative-evidence example ("How does Maria see her neighbourhood?")** is compelling but the images (`image.png`, `image-1.png`) are not described in the markdown — students reading the source cannot follow this example.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Organise this session around "how will you be judged?":
1. Qualitative quality criteria (Small's six) applied to students' own projects
2. Quantitative quality: classifier validation + measurement validity
3. Mixed methods quality: how the combination adds value (confirmatory/complementary payoff)
4. Exam prototype walkthrough — what an examiner is looking for section by section
