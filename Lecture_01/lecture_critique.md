# Lecture 1 Critique — What is Mixed Digital Methods?

**Syllabus date:** 8 April
**Syllabus title:** What is mixed digital methods?

---

## What Works

- The "start with the end" move (exam first, then theory) is pedagogically strong — students immediately know what they are building toward.
- The three-axis framing (data / methods / analysis) for digital methods, mixed methods, and mixed digital methods is elegant and returns consistently throughout the course.
- Worked examples from the instructor's own research (incels, Ukrainian brigades) are concrete and motivating.

---

## Critiques

1. **Slide bloat from progressive disclosure.** The three-axis definition is built across ~10 near-identical slides. This creates bulk without adding content. Consolidate into 3 slides that build simultaneously.

2. **"Methods of the medium" is introduced but dropped.** Rogers's digital methods concept is mentioned but the distinction from simply "using digital data" is never resolved. Either elaborate on how this shapes the course or remove it.

3. **Internal course outline is out of sync with the syllabus.** The outline slide inside the lecture (showing 6 broad sessions) does not match the 8-lecture syllabus schedule.

4. **Small (2011) typology is underused.** The distinction between mixed data-collection studies and mixed data-analysis studies is quoted but not applied to the course structure or the examples. This typology deserves more airtime given it is core reading.

5. **CSS typo in header.** The `!im  portant` (with internal space) in the marp style block is a rendering bug — the font override will not apply.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Open with a compelling real social puzzle that requires mixed methods to solve — something students recognise (AI discourse, climate activism online, political polarisation). Show why neither pure qualitative nor pure quantitative works. Then introduce the three-axis framework as the solution architecture. End with the course workflow as a concrete roadmap that previews each subsequent lecture.

---

## Readings

**Keep:**
- Blok, Carlsen & Albris (2022) — core framing
- Small (2011) — typological foundation; use the full typology more explicitly in slides

**Add:**
- Rogers, Richard. "Doing Digital Methods." SAGE (2019), ch. 1 (~15 pp.) — properly grounds the "methods of the medium" concept if retained
- boyd, danah & Kate Crawford. "Critical Questions for Big Data." *Information, Communication & Society* 15.5 (2012): 662–679 — motivates the *why* of mixed approaches from a critical angle; very readable and short
