The course is built around one project

The exam is a 25-page mixed digital methods project

Students collect digital data, answer a self-chosen research question, and combine qualitative and quantitative analysis.

The project should include
  • a clear research design
  • a datasite and data collection strategy
  • qualitative analysis
  • quantitative analysis
  • integration across methods

The exam rewards integration, not separate mini-studies

Exam focus

◎

The key standard

The strongest projects do not place qual and quant side by side. They make them inform each other.

A good project has a recognisable structure

Must-haves
  • datasite description informed by netnography
  • data collection account
  • research design
  • qualitative text analysis
  • quantitative text analysis
  • validated computational classification
Typical paper architecture
  1. Introduction
  2. Background and datasite
  3. Theory and literature
  4. Design
  5. Data collection
  6. Methods
  7. Analysis
  8. Conclusion

Good projects turn a concrete case into an analytical question

Example: “Incels and Extremism”

Research question: What tenets in incel culture resemble the structure of extremist ideology?

The point of the example is not the topic itself. It is the design logic connecting question, concept, data, and analysis.

A convincing project connects datasite and collection strategy

Mixed work means patterns and passages must speak to each other

Qualitative evidence

“I am the worst of the worst. I don’t deserve to mate and breed.”

This kind of passage gives interpretive depth that a pattern alone cannot supply.

Digital methods begin with digital traces

Digital methods repurpose platform traces as research data

The object is not “the internet” in general. The object is the traces, rankings, links, metadata, and interactions generated inside specific media environments.

Four examples of traces
  • likes and reactions
  • comments and replies
  • hashtags and links
  • rankings, recommendations, and metrics

Digital methods also learn from the medium itself

The method point

Platforms already sort, rank, recommend, cluster, and connect content.

Digital methods learn from those built-in operations instead of treating them as irrelevant noise.

Digital methods interpret patterns rather than stopping at them

Core move

Quantitative patterns become useful when they are interpreted as medium-specific social formations.

Mixed methods matter because digital data are mixed already

In terms of data

Digital material can be qualitative in form and quantitative in scale.

In terms of methods

Projects often need both contextual observation and scalable collection.

In terms of analysis

Interpretive and statistical work both become weaker when they are isolated from each other.

Mixed digital methods combine three things at once

01

Repurpose and combine qualitative and quantitative digital traces

02

Combine methods such as netnography and web scraping

03

Combine interpretive and analytical forms of computer-assisted analysis

⇄

Make the three layers inform each other

The point is not better platform engineering

The goal is to investigate wider cultural dynamics

Digital methods use medium-specific operations to study public life, discourse, organization, and social worlds. They do not aim to optimize the platforms themselves.

Practical warning

If a project only collects platform data without asking what those traces mean in context, it stops too early.

The course treats the class as a research collective

The lecture course is designed to help projects mature in public

The class works as a collective, while exercise groups function as topical clusters made up of smaller independent project teams.

Weekly routines keep projects moving

Exercise classes
  • one-slide update every Tuesday
  • group work on milestones
  • feedback on project development
  • occasional small methods exercises
Supervision sessions
  • six sessions per group
  • 25 minutes each
  • short progress update plus current challenge
  • focused help on design and analysis

The first takeaway is simple

Takeaway

A strong mixed digital methods project links traces, context, and interpretation

That is the standard the rest of the course will keep returning to.
