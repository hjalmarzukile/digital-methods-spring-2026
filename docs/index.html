About

Course description

Using digital methods is a specific approach to doing digital social research. In digital methods, the focus is placed on the digital media contexts where data is generated as a by-product of social interaction, and on new ways of combining quantitative and qualitative methods of digital inquiry and analysis. This course provides students with practical skills in implementing three sets of computer-assisted qualitative methods: exploratory network analysis, digital online ethnography, and content analysis. As such, it supplements the various quantitative techniques taught in other courses on the degree programme, and provides tools for mixing qualitative methods with textual and/or visual quantitative data into qualitative-quantitative social-science analyses. Students train these skills by conducting their own integrated mapping of a public issue, involving networks, ideas, and behaviour across individual and organisational levels and across multiple digital platforms.

Learning outcomes

What you will learn

Knowledge

  • Basic techniques, use scenarios, and validity criteria of computer-assisted qualitative methods — digital ethnography, content analysis, and exploratory network analysis
  • Procedures, potentials, and pitfalls of combining qualitative and quantitative data sources in integrated quali-quantitative ways
  • The relationship between digital methods' media-context emphasis and broader questions of bias in social data science

Skills

  • Design appropriate semantic categories for qualitative content analysis, including for subsequent machine learning
  • Extract and communicate patterns of networks, ideas, and behaviour using the appropriate qualitative method(s)
  • Combine qualitative data with a quantitative data source into comprehensive social analyses

Competencies

  • Evaluate a social data problem from both qualitative and quantitative perspectives
  • Design and implement digital ethnography campaigns, exploratory network analysis, and content analysis
  • Produce persuasive quali-quantitative reports combining qualitative and quantitative sources of data, for a range of organisational use scenarios

Exam

Group-based oral exam with a prior written assignment in groups of 3–4 students. The written assignment must describe method accounts, forms of analyses, and formulate and evaluate an explicit research question. It may include code, field notes, and data visualisations.

  • 3-person group: max 20 standard pages, 40 min oral (30 min examination)
  • 4-person group: max 25 standard pages, 45 min oral
  • All aids allowed · 7-point grading scale · No external censorship
  • Written assignment hand-in: 12 June
  • Oral exams: week 25
~400 pages required literature + 80 pages self-selected
6 supervision sessions per group
4 milestones across the course
