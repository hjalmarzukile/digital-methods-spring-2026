## What Works

- The Carlsen & Ralund (2022) framing of "computer-led to computer-assisted" text analysis is the theoretical spine of the entire text analysis arc. It is well-chosen and the slides reference it well.
- The open coding section grounded in Timmermans & Tavory is solid and methodologically serious.
- The in-class coding session with two cases (trip reports; doomsday in prepping communities) is excellent pedagogically — hands-on, grounded in real data.
- The workflow overview (discovery → grounding → classification → measurement) is clear.

---

## Critiques

1. **Missing conceptual foundation.** The old `Lecture 8/` folder (text as data, deductive vs. inductive approaches, grounded theory, computational grounded theory) contains essential background content that was previously delivered before this lecture. Without it, students may not understand what they are discovering *for*. Absorb a compressed version of that background as the opening section of this lecture.

2. **In-class cases are named but absent from the slides.** The two open coding cases ("Trip reports" and "Doomsday in prepping") are listed but the actual documents do not appear in the slides — they are presumably separate handouts. Ensure these are prepared, accessible, and clearly referenced.

3. **Word2vec/keyword expansion is technically thin.** The most demanding technical concept in the lecture gets minimal treatment. At minimum, add a diagram or intuitive analogy: "words that appear in the same contexts as your seed words."

4. **Karell & Freedman (2019) at 27 pages is demanding.** Students risk reading it without extracting the methodological lessons. The slides should deconstruct the pipeline explicitly rather than expecting students to extract it themselves.

5. **Unsupervised methods listed without selection guidance.** BERTopic, LDA, Structural Topic Model, HSBM, semantic network analysis, word counts are all mentioned but no guidance is given on when to use which. Add a brief comparison.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Structure the lecture explicitly around the workflow:
1. You have data — now what? (brief conceptual framing, absorbing Lecture 8 background)
2. The discovery phase — what unsupervised methods give you and what they do not
3. Open coding — what human reading adds that machines cannot
4. How they work together (Carlsen & Ralund's computer-assisted model as the synthesis)

Use the Carlsen & Ralund diagram as the spine throughout.
