# Lecture 2 Critique — Research Question and Mixed Research Design

**Syllabus date:** 10 April
**Syllabus title:** Research question and mixed research designs

---

## What Works

- The question typology (what/how/where/why/who/when) with worked examples drawn from the instructor's own research (incels, Ukrainian brigades, refugee solidarity) is excellent — grounded, concrete, and directly usable.
- The casing concept (moving from empirical to theoretical case and vice versa) is underused in methods courses and genuinely valuable.
- The sequential analysis design diagram (`analysisdesign_2.png`) functions well as a course-level roadmap.

---

## Critiques

1. **Broken image slide.** The "Mixed Method Motives" slide repeats `![alt text](image-1.png)` eight times. This is a corrupted slide that will render as a wall of identical images. Fix or remove.

2. **Confirmatory/complementary over-repeated.** This framework is introduced here, then again in Lecture 4 (data collection design), and again in Lecture 8 (analysis design). Either build it progressively with genuine new depth at each stage, or introduce it once and use forward references.

3. **Grigoropoulou & Small (2022) is misplaced.** This reading argues for the value of offline qualitative methods in digital research — a strong point, but it sits awkwardly before students have chosen datasites. Its payoff moment is later (Lecture 3+) when they are actually deciding between online and offline grounding. Consider moving it.

4. **"Offline vs online grounding" slide is underdeveloped.** This is an important decision point for students but is handled in two sentences without resolution. It needs a decision framework: when do you stay online, when do you go offline?

5. **Broken image reference in slides.** One slide references `../lecture 2 Design and Research Question/analysisdesign_2.png` — this folder does not exist in the current directory structure. The image path needs to be corrected to a local reference.

---

## Alternative Organisation

Consider splitting this lecture more clearly:
- First half: research questions (casing, question types, rules for good questions)
- Second half: the sequential analysis design as the course's master template — use the design diagram as the backbone and explain each step as a preview of the next seven weeks

This gives students a cognitive map they can return to throughout the course.

---

## Readings

**Keep:**
- Frederiksen, Toubøl & Carlsen — core design chapter
- Grigoropoulou & Small (2022) — but consider moving to Lecture 3 where its argument about offline grounding is more immediately applicable

**Add or substitute:**
- Creswell & Plano Clark, "Designing and Conducting Mixed Methods Research" ch. 3 (typology of designs, ~20 pp.) — more internationally recognised mixed methods typology
- Timmermans & Tavory, "Abductive Analysis" ch. 1 (~20 pp.) — introduces abduction early as the epistemological spine connecting qualitative and quantitative work in this course
